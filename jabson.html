Constellations of Freedom

Shakespearean Sonnet

A bright star in space seeking attention
Slowly setting minute embers ablaze
My plans as cryptic as death’s ascension
Wait, be as still as water in the frays

A hazed symphony plays at my demise
At death, the universe, it sings for me!
A key, comparable to our life’s size
Each second can’t be wasted freely

Where are my fellow stars in this cosmic ocean?
Those who aspire to split seas colored bright red
Will our radiant gleam shimmer like a corrosion?
Or will the night be filled with dread and the tears we shed?

Regardless, Ill stand proud with my brothers
With passion-filled air no one will dare to smother
