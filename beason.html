The Soloist's Partner

Petrarchan Sonnet

Roses dance fervently at night,
Swaying to the breeze of love.
Thorns holding up the petals above,
The breeze turning into a tornado of spite.

The thorns hold the petals tight,
In the end, one of the petals fall off,
It grasps that it can fly as freely as a dove.
The petal realized too late, it was sliced.

The nights of dancing and swaying,
Left too many marks on the petal.
Now it’s free, life within no longer fading,

Living in a whole new different level.
The nights are not worth remembering,
It doesn’t need a partner to be special.

The Door I Opened

Shakespearean Sonnet

Staring at those eyes so enchanting,
My heart pumps faster by the minute.
My love for you never ending,
I can’t find the words to express it.

Our souls intertwined with one another,
We found each other happily interlocked.
Amidst of this lovely night I felt a shudder,
Coming from the door, someone knocked.

A man stood there looking at you,
Explain not, my last line has been drawn.
I gave you a lot of chances to,
Make me less heartbroken, less forlorn.

Had I realized sooner you and me,
were never meant to be.
