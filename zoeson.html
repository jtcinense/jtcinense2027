Tear-Speckled Skies

Shakespearean Sonnet

Your frustrations, lachrymose, and lament
Slowly trickle down your white tinted face
Fragmented paint, chipping off like cement
Tear out the mask, wipe it off, leave no trace

Take in the view beyond your comfort zone
Can’t you see the cosmos’ effervescence?
In this vast universe, you’re not alone
For the stars are here, amidst the darkness

Be not afraid to spread your wings, my dear
You have already come a long journey
Don’t let the uncertainty interfere
For your hesitance makes your path murky

Let yourself be free, let yourself flourish
Gaze upon the skies, and please make a wish
